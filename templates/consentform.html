You are being invited to take part in a research study conducted by the researchers named above. Below is detailed information for you to consider when determining whether or not to participate. Carefully consider all of this information (and the information provided above) and ask any questions you may have about it before deciding whether to participate or not.

Key Information for You to Consider

·                Voluntary Consent: You are being asked to volunteer for a research study. It is your choice whether to participate or not. There will be no penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled if you choose not to participate or to discontinue participation.

·                Purpose: The purpose of this research is assess aspects of human visual perception, including contour detection and other mid-level visual and attention skills.  You are being invited to participate because you are in the tested age range. It is expected that approximately 200 individuals will be participating in this research.

·                Procedures and Activities: You will be asked to respond to a set of images of grayscale patches, and detect which side has more patches.  The task will become progressively more difficult.  You will respond using a keyboard.  This research will take place in Science IV, rm 377b.   

·                Duration: Your time commitment will be approximately 15-60 minutes.

·                Risks: Some of the foreseeable risks or discomforts of your participation include dryness of the eyes from looking hard at a screen for a significant time period.  Clearly, the risk of coming to the lab during the Covid pandemic represents a significant additional risk.  We will mitigate this risk by having you alone in the testing room, and by disinfecting all appropriate surfaces, but there is a risk associated with any in-person appointment that we cannot completely eliminate.  We are using your SONA-provided email so that you can be contacted prior to your appointment time (so as to minimize your time in the building).  We will delete your email as soon as the appointment is over, but there is some risk of that information being disclosed.

·                Benefits: You will receive SONA credit for your participation; typically ½ of a credit.  We do not foresee other benefits beyond learning firsthand how such an experiment is conducted. 

·                Alternatives: Participation is voluntary and the only alternative is to not participate.

What happens to the information collected for this research?

Information collected as part of this research will be presented at professional conferences and published in research reports. Your identify will never be part of any such report, however.

How will my privacy and data confidentiality be protected?

We will take measures to protect your privacy including deleting your email as soon as we have completed your appointment, and by not collecting any information regarding your identity.  We will also use a secure computer for storing data.

The Qualtrics survey that you are taking now is a Binghamton University-approved survey platform and we anticipate that your participation in this survey presents no greater risk than everyday use of the Internet.

Please note that email communication is neither private nor secure. Though we are taking precautions to protect your privacy, you should be aware that information sent through e-mail could be read by a third party. 

Despite these precautions, we can never fully guarantee your privacy or the confidentiality of all study information. Individuals and organizations that conduct or monitor this research may be permitted access to inspect the research records, including accessing your private information.  These individuals and organizations include: approved study team members, and the Binghamton University Institutional Review Board (IRB).

What if I want to stop participating in this research?

Taking part in this research study is your decision. Your participation is voluntary. You do not have to take part in this study, but if you do, you can choose not to participate in any study activity or to completely withdraw at any point without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.  Your decision whether or not to participate will not affect your relationship with the researcher(s) or Binghamton University.
